Every Christmas I play chess with my dad. Here are my 3 takeaways from our games that can be applied to real life too.
1. Short term vs Long term strategies.
If you plan only for the next 1-2 moves, it will have small impact on the game. If you plan for many more moves ahead, you can reach way bigger goals (eg in terms of the game: controlling strategic parts of board, paralyzing opponent or eliminating his attacking power, launch a fatal offensive). This is so true for life.
2. Don't do things randomly. Follow your goals.
Your moves on the chess board are limited. Same as your life. All your fugures are your resources to achieve your higher goals. Sometimes there is no other way than sacrifying some them in order to reach your goals. Don't be afraid of that. Don't be afraid of that in real life too.
3. Be unpredictable.
- you can play in two ways. Either you allow your opponent to read all your moves and intentions. The other way is to play unpredictable and when opponent understands your strategies, it will be too late for him to stop you. You can think of that if you work in competitive environments.
4. Be adaptable.
Conditions in the game change dynamically. The best strategy may not lead to desired gains any more. Same as life. That means you need to reevaluate your strategies all the time. Have alternative plans ready to be deployed if the previous ones stop leading you to desired goals.
